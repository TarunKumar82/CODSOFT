Subhas Chandra Bose

The Forgotten Hero

Here's a timeline of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose:

  • 1897 - Born in Cuttack, Orissa, India on January 23rd.
  • 1919 - Passed the Indian Civil Service exam, but turned down the job because he did not want to work under the British.
  • 1921 - Joined the Indian National Congress and became involved in the independence movement.
  • 1923 - Elected leader of the All India Youth Council and secretary of the Bengal State Congress.
  • 1925 - Arrested by the British for his involvement in revolutionary activities.
  • 1927 - Released from prison and began working with Jawaharlal Nehru.
  • 1930 - Elected mayor of Calcutta.
  • 1938 - Became president of the Indian National Congress.
  • 1939 - Organized the All India Forward Bloc, a socialist faction within the Congress.
  • 1941 - Escaped from India and traveled to Germany and Japan in search of support for India's independence.
  • 1943 - Founded the Indian National Army (INA), a military force composed of Indian prisoners of war and civilians living in Southeast Asia.
  • 1944 -Led the INA in an unsuccessful invasion of India.
  • 1945 - Disappeared in a plane crash on August 18th. Bose's legacy is complex and controversial. He is widely admired for his patriotism and his willingness to fight for India's independence. However, his wartime alliances with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan have also been criticized.
